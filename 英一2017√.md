# text 4 美最高法院推翻前弗吉尼亚州州长贪腐定罪判决
## 1
- 在一次罕见的一致裁决中，美国最高法院推翻了前弗吉尼亚州州长罗伯特·麦克唐纳的腐败定罪。 在一次罕见的一致裁决中，美国最高法院推翻了一项腐败定罪(关于前弗吉尼亚州州长-罗伯特·麦克唐纳)。<br>In a rare unanimous ruling, the US Supreme Court has overturned the  corruption conviction of a former Virginia governor, Robert McDonnell. 
- 但在这样做的同时，它持批评态度对于其道德(有关他的行为) ，包括接受礼物如劳力士手表和法拉利汽车来自一家公司(寻求通道向政府)。<br>But it did  so while holding  its nose at the ethics of his conduct, which  included  accepting  gifts such as a Rolex watch and a Ferrari automobile from a  company  seeking access to government.
## 2
- 高等法院的裁决指出，法官在麦克唐纳先生审判中的未能告知陪审团,它只审视他的“官方行为”,或者前州长的决策在“具体”和“未定”事务(相关于他的职业方面)<br>The high court’s decision said  the judge  in Mr. McDonnell’s trial failed  to tell a jury that it must look only at his “official acts,” or the  former  governor’s  decisions on “specific” and “unsettled” issues related to his duties.
## 3
- Merely helping a gift-giver gain access to other officials, unless done with  clear intent to pressure those officials, is not corruption, the justices found.仅仅帮助送礼者获得接触机会向其他官员，除非做得很明显的打算去施加压力对这些官员，否则并不是腐败,自这些法官们发现。
## 4
- 法院已经表示，接受好处为了开后门是 “令人嫌恶” 和 “恶心”的。<br>The court did suggest that accepting favors in return for opening doors is “distasteful” and “nasty.” 
- 但根据反贿赂法律，证据必须组成于具体的利益，如一些批准( 关于合同或规定)<br>But under anti-bribery laws, proof must be made of  concrete benefits, such as approval of a contract or regulation. 
- 仅简单地安排一个会面、打一个电话或主持一个活动, 并不是一种 “官方行为”。<br>Simply arranging a meeting, making a phone call, or hosting an event is not an “official act”. 
## 5
- 法院的裁决在法律上是合理的在定义一种偏袒行为是 (那种非刑事犯罪的)。<br>The court’s ruling is legally sound in defining a kind of favoritism that is not criminal.
- 被选举出来的领导人必须被允许帮助支持者处理官僚问题 (不再害怕被起诉因为贿赂。 <br> Elected leaders must be allowed to help supporters deal with  bureaucratic problems without fear of prosecution for bribery. 
- “基本的契约有关代表性政府的 ”，首席大法官约翰·罗伯茨给法院写道，“假设公职官员会听取他们的选民意见并行之以他们的关怀。”<br>“The basic compact underlying representative government,” wrote Chief Justice John Roberts for the court, “assumes that public officials  will hear from  their constituents and act on  their concerns.” 
## 6
- 但这一裁决强化了公民和他们的民选代表，而不是法院，确保平等接触政府的需要。但这一裁决强调了其必要性(即由公民和他们选举的代表（而非法院）来确保平等地获得政府服务)<br>But the ruling reinforces the need for citizens and their elected representatives, not the courts, to ensure equality of access to government. 
- 官员不应被允许表现出偏袒在提供信息或安排会面时, 仅仅因为某个人或团体提供了竞选捐款或个人礼物。 <br>Officials must not be allowed to play favorites in providing information or in  arranging  meetings simply because an individual or group provides a campaign donation or a personal gift.
- 这种正直需要政府透明度方面的严格执法，如官方会议记录、游说规则以及每位民选领导人财富来源的信息。这种类型的诚信要求有良好执行的法律(有关政府透明度)，例如记录(如官方会议)、规则(如游说)以及信息(即每位当选领导人来源关于财富)。<br>This type of integrity requires well-enforced laws in government transparency, such as records of official meetings, rules on lobbying, and  information about each elected leader’s source of wealth.  
## 7
- 偏袒(在官方通道中的)可能会加剧公众的感知对腐败。<br>Favoritism in official access can fan public perceptions of corruption. 
- 但这并不总是腐败。<br>But it is not always corruption.
