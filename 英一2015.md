# text1 欧洲王室是否行将就木
## 1
- King Juan Carlos of Spain once insisted “kings don’t abdicate, they die in their sleep.”<br>西班牙国王胡安·卡洛斯曾坚称“国王不会退位，他们去世于他们的睡梦中。”
- But embarrassing scandals and the popularity of the republican left in the recent Euro-elections have forced him to eat his words and stand down. <br>但是尴尬的丑闻和这种受欢迎程度关于共和党左翼(在近期欧洲选举中)迫使他食言并下台。
- So, does the Spanish crisis suggest that monarchy is seeing its last days?<br>那么，是否西班牙危机暗示着君主制正在经历它最后的日子？
- Does that mean the writing is on the wall for all European royals, with their magnificent uniforms and majestic lifestyles? <br>这是否意味着，这种书写已经显现在墙上对所有欧洲王室来说，连同他们华丽的制服和庄严的生活方式？
## 2
- The Spanish case provides arguments both for and against monarchy. <br>西班牙的情形提供了依据对双方(即支持或反对君主制).
- When public opinion is particularly polarised, as it was following the end of the Franco regime, monarchs can rise above “mere”politics and “embody”a spirit of national unity. <br>当公众的观点极其地两极分化时，就像刚结束时那样在佛朗哥政权，君主能够超越“纯粹的”政治，并“体现"一种精神即民族团结。
## 3
- It is this apparent transcendence of politics that explains monarchs’continuing popularity as heads of state. <br>正是这种明显的超越对于政治，解释了君主的持续受欢迎度去作国家元首。
- And so, the Middle East excepted, Europe is the most monarch-infested region in the world, with 10 kingdoms (not counting Vatican city and Andorra). <br>因此，中东除外，欧洲是君主最密集的地区在世界上，这里有十个王国(不计入梵蒂冈城和安道尔)。
- But unlike their absolutist counterparts in the Gulf and Asia, most royal families have survived because they allow voters to avoid the difficult search for a non-controversial but respected public figure. <br>但是不同于他们的专制主义君主(在海湾地区和亚洲地区)，大多数王室家庭能够幸存下来，是因为他们使选民避免费力寻找一个没有争议却受人敬重的公众人物。
## 4
- Even so, kings and queens undoubtedly have a downside. <br>即便如此，国王和女王无疑都有其不利方面。
- Symbolic of national unity as they claim to be, their very history –and sometimes the way they behave today –embodies outdated and indefensible privileges and inequalities. <br>象征着民族团结正如他们声称的，但他们的历史-有时这种方式(他们所为所为)如今一体现着过时且不合理的特权和不平等现象。
- At a time when Thomas Piketty and other economists are warning of rising inequality and the increasing power of inherited wealth, it is bizarre that wealthy aristocratic families should still be the symbolic heart of modern democratic states. <br>在这个时候,当托马斯.皮凯蒂和其他经济学家正警告这种不断加剧的不平等现象和不断扩大的财富继承权问题时，这是十分怪异的,也就是富有的贵族家庭竟依旧是象征的核心在现代民主国家。
## 5
- The most successful monarchies strive to abandon or hide their old aristocratic ways.<br>最成功的君主们努力去抛弃或隐藏他们先前的贵族生活方式。
- Princes and princesses have day-jobs and ride bicycles, not horses (or helicopters).<br>王子和公主有日常工作和骑自行车,而不是骑马(或直升机)。
- Even so, these are wealthy families who party with the international 1%, and media intrusiveness makes it increasingly difficult to maintain the right image. <br>即便如此，他们仍是富裕家庭位居全球前1%，而且媒体的干涉使他们越来越困难去维持正确的形象。
## 6
- While Europe’s monarchies will no doubt be smart enough to survive for some time to come, it is the British royals who have most to fear from the Spanish example. <br>欧洲的君主们无疑是很聪明的足以幸存一段时间在未来，这也是英国王室却最恐惧的来自西班牙的例子。
## 7
- It is only the Queen who has preserved the monarchy’s reputation with her rather ordinary (if well-heeled)granny style. <br>这只有女王保住了君主的声誉,以她的相对平凡的(尽管穿着相当考究的)老奶奶风格。
- The danger will come with Charles, who has both an expensive taste of lifestyle and a pretty hierarchical view of the world.<br>危险降临至查尔斯王子，他同时有着奢华的品位对于生活和颇具等级色彩的世界观
- He has failed to understand that monarchies have largely survived because they provide a service –as non-controversial and non-political heads of state. <br>他错误地理解了, 君主制很大程度上能幸存下来,是因为他们提供了一种服务,即充当无争议、非政治性的国家元首。
- Charles ought to know that as English history shows, it is kings, not republicans,who are the monarchy’s worst enemies. <br>查尔斯王子理应知道，正如英国历史所昭示:是国王，而不是共和党人，才是君主制最大的敌人(enemies)。

## 1
- Two years ago, Rupert Murdoch’s daughter, Elisabeth, spoke of the “unsettling dearth of integrity across so many of our institutions”.<br>两年前，鲁伯特.默多克的女儿伊丽莎白,谈到:“令人担忧的缺失问题关于诚信存在于许多我们的机构中”。
- Integrity had collapsed, she argued, because of a collective acceptance that the only “sorting mechanism”in society should be profit and the market. <br>诚信已经沦丧了，她说,因为一个普遍的接受认为唯一的“分选机制在社会中”应该是利益和市场。
- But “it’s us, human beings, we the people who create the society we want, not profit.”<br>然而，“是我们人类自己,创造了这种社会我们想要的,而不是利益”。
## 2
- Driving her point home, she continued:“It’s increasingly apparent that the absence of purpose, of a moral language within government, media or business could become one of the most dangerous goals for capitalism and freedom.”<br>为了阐释清楚她的的观点，她继续说道:“这越来越明显的是，政府、媒体或企业内部目的不明确和道德语言的缺失可能会成为资本主义与自由最危险的目标之一。”
- This same absence of moral purpose was wounding companies such as News International, she thought, making it more likely that it would lose its way as it had with widespread illegal telephone hacking. <br>这种同样的缺失关于道德目标正伤害着那些公司诸如新闻国际这样的,她认为，使那些公司更有可能误入歧途像它曾经一样因大规模非法的电话窃听。
## 3
- As the hacking trial concludes –finding guilty one ex-editor of the News of the World, Andy Coulson, for conspiring to hack phones, and finding his predecessor, Rebekah Brooks, innocent of the same charge –the wider issue of dearth of integrity still stands. <br>尽管窃听案审判结束了,被判有罪对一个前任编辑关于《世界新闻报》,安迪.库尔森因,策划了电话窃听案，但发现他的前任编辑丽贝卡.布鲁克斯,却被判无罪面对同样的指控, 但更大范围的问题关于确失诚信仍然存在。

- This is hacking on an industrial scale, as was acknowledged by Glenn Mulcaire, the man hired by the News of the World in 2001 to be the point person for phone hacking. <br>这是一次窃听事件在一个工业规模级别的,正如其所承认的那样由格伦.穆尔凯尔,这个男人受雇于《世界新闻报》在2001年，并成为焦点人物因电话窃听案。
