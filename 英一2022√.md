# text1-当塑料弥足珍贵之时
## 1

- 人们经常抱怨塑料太过于耐用<br>People often complain that plastics are too durable. 
- 水瓶，购物包和其他的垃圾乱扔在地球上-从珠穆朗玛峰到马里亚纳海沟，因为塑料是无处不在的，并且不会降解轻易地。<br>Water bottles, shopping bags, and other trash litter the planet, from Mount Everest to the Mariana Trench, because plastics are everywhere and don’t break down easily. 
- 但是一些塑料的材料会变化随着时间推移.<br>But some plastic materials change over time. 
- 他们会破裂和卷曲。<br>They crack and frizzle. 
- 他们会哭出(渗出)添加剂。	<br>They “weep”out additives. 
- 他们会融进污泥里。<br>They melt into sludge. 
- 所有的这一切都会创造巨大的头痛烦恼对那些机构，比如博物馆,正努力去保护文化性的重要物品。<br>All of which creates huge headaches for institutions, such as museums, trying to preserve culturally important objects. 
- 多重多样的塑料物品那些处于危险之中的是令人眼花缭乱：早期的收音机，先锋派的雕塑品，赛璐璐动画剧照来自于迪士尼电影，第一颗人造的心脏。<br>The variety of plastic objects at risk is dizzying:early radios, avant-garde sculptures, celluloid animation stills from Disney films, the first artificial heart. 
## 2 
- 某些手工艺品是格外的脆弱，因为一些先驱者在塑料艺术中并不总是知道如何去混合各种成分适当地,西亚.范.奥斯腾这样说，一个聚合物化学家,直到退休于前些年，他工作了数十年在文化遗产机构,荷兰的<br>Certain artifacts are especially vulnerable because some pioneers in plastic art didn’t always know how to mix ingredients properly, says Thea van Oosten, a polymer chemist who, until retiring a few years ago, worked for decades at the Cultural Heritage Agency of the Netherlands. 
- “它像烤一个蛋糕，如果你没有精确的用量，它就会出错”他说。<br>“It’s like baking a cake:If you don’t have exact amounts, it goes wrong,”she says. 
- 这个物品你所做的已经是一个定时炸弹了。<br>“The object you make is already a time bomb.”
## 3
- 并且有时这不是艺术家的错误。<br>And sometimes, it’s not the artist’s fault. 
- 在1960年，意大利的艺术家Picro Gilardi就开始去创作了数百件光亮的色彩夺目的泡沫艺术品。<br>In the 1960s, the Italian artist Picro Gilardi began to create hundreds of bright, colorful foam pieces. 
- 那些艺术品里包括小床关于玫瑰的,和其他的项目以及几十个自然地毯-巨大的矩形被装饰上泡沫南瓜，白菜，和西瓜<br>Those pieces included small beds of roses and other items as well as a few dozen “nature carpets”—large rectangles decorated with foam pumpkins, cabbages, and watermelons. 
- 他想要参观者来回走动于地毯上，这意味着它们不得不是耐用的。<br>He wanted viewers to walk around on the carpets—which meant they had to be durable. 
## 4 		
- 不幸的是，聚氨酯泡沫他所用的是本身就不稳定的。<br>Unfortunately, the polyurethane foam he used is inherently unstable. 
- 它尤其地脆弱对于光照的伤害，到了1990年代中期，杰拉尔迪的南瓜，玫瑰和其他的雕像都正在碎裂和坍塌<br>It’s especially vulnerable to light damage, and by the mid-1990s, Gilardi’s pumpkins, roses, and other figures were spitting and crumbling. 
- 博物馆锁住他们中的一些放在暗处里。<br>Museums locked some of them away in the dark. 
## 5
- 所以范.奥斯腾和她的同事们就致力于保护杰拉尔迪的雕塑们<br>So van Oosten and her colleagues worked to preserve Gilardi’s sculptures. 
- 他们注入进一些(雕塑)中一种具有稳定和加固作用的化学物质。<br>They infused some with stabilizing and consolidating chemicals. 
- 范.奥斯腾称这些化学物为"防晒霜"，因为他们的目标是去阻止进一步的光照伤害和修复被用坏的聚合物纤维<br>Van Oosten calls those chemicals “sunscreens”because their goal was to prevent further light damage and rebuild worn polymer fibers.
- 她很自豪，数个雕塑品已经甚至进行展出了又，尽管有时是在保护罩的下方。<br>She is proud that several sculptures have even gone on display again, albeit sometimes beneath protective cases.
## 6 
- 尽管有成功的故事像范.奥斯腾这样的，但这种保护关于塑料文物将可能变得更加艰难<br>Despite success stories like van Oosten’s, preservation of plastics will likely get harder. 
- 老的物品继续在变坏。<br>Old objects continue to deteriorate.
- 更糟糕的是，生物降解塑料,那些被设计为可被分解的,是越来越普遍。<br>Worse, biodegradable plastics ,designed to disintegrate, are increasingly common
## 7
- 并且更多的东西处于危境而不是个别的物件<br>And more is at stake here than individual objects.
- 杰奥娜.丽雅.费雷拉，一个助理教授关于保护和修复的在NOVA大学的科学技术系，写道:考古学家第一次定义伟大材料时代在人类历史上(石器时代，铁器时代和更多)-在检查文物之后从博物馆那。<br>Joana Lia Ferreira, an assistant professor of conservation and restoration at the NOVA School of Science and Technology, notes that archaeologists first defined the great material ages of human history—Stone Age, Iron Age, and so on—after examining artifacts in museums. 
- 我们现在生活在一个时代关于塑料，她说，并且我们决定去收藏什么今天，我们决定去保护什么…都将有一个强烈的影响对于在未来我们会看到什么。<br>We now live in an age of plastic, she says, “and what we decide to collect today, what we decide to preserve...will have a strong impact on how in the future we’ll be seen.”

## 2

## 1 
- 个人申诉条款在新西兰雇佣关系法规中的阻止雇佣者解雇一个员工在没有好的理由情况下<br>The personal grievance provisions of New Zealand’s Employment Relations Act 2000 (ERA)prevent an employer from firing an employee without good cause.
- 换句话说，解雇必须是有正当理由的<br>Instead, dismissals must be justified.
- 雇主必须同时展示原因，又行动以程序化的正规方式<br>Employers must both show cause and act in a procedurally fair way.
## 2
- 个人申诉程序被设计去保护这些工作关于普通工作者免受不正当理由的解雇<br>Personal grievance procedures were designed to guard the jobs of ordinary workers from “unjustified dismissals”.
- 前提是普通法律合同缺乏充分的保障对于工作者免受随意的指挥被管理者<br>The premise was that the common law of contract lacked sufficient safeguards for workers against arbitrary conduct by management.
- 早已一去不返的是那些日子，当一个老板可以轻易地给一名员工合同通知。"<br>Long gone are the days when a boss could simply give an employee contractual notice.
## 3 
- 但这些条款会创造了诸多困难对企业,当应用到高薪管理者和经理时<br>But these provisions create difficulties for businesses when applied to highly paid managers and executives.

- 然而平庸已不再足以去证明其正当性对于解雇。<br>Yet mediocrity is no longer enough to justify a dismissal.

- 的确，在一个国际角度下的新英格兰生产力驳论中，生产力委员会指出低质量的管理能力是一个原因对于这个国家的糟糕的生产力增长记录.<br>Indeed, in “An International Perspective on New Zealand’s Productivity Paradox”(2014), the Productivity Commission singled out the low quality of managerial capabilities as a cause of the country’s poor productivity growth record.
## 5 
- 同样不能的是让高薪管理者他们自己免疫这种伤害来自雇佣保护法中的不正当的解雇程序<br>Nor are highly paid managers themselves immune from the harm caused by the ERA’s unjustified dismissal procedures.

- 这使其变得更加艰难对于平庸的管理者去获得雇佣<br>This makes it harder for the marginal manager to gain employment.
- 并且公司支付给员工更少，因为公司要承担这个负担关于雇佣安排出现错误<br>And firms pay staff less because firms carry the burden of the employment arrangement going wrong.
## 6 
- 社会也受过伤害(来自于过度雇佣保护)<br>Society also suffers from excessive employment protections.
- 严格的工作解雇法规负面地影响生产力增长，并且同时妨碍繁荣和整体的福祉。<br>Stringent job dismissal regulations adversely affect productivity growth and hamper both prosperity and overall well-being.
