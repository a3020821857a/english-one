- Many French politicians and media outlets have referred to this as a “GAFA tax,”meaning that it is designed to apply primarily to companies such as Google, Apple, Facebook and Amazon—in other words, multinational tech companies based in the United States.<br>许多法国政治家和媒体机构称呼它为“GAFA 税”，这意味着它旨在适用主要这些公司,比如Google、Apple、Facebook 和Amazon ——换句话说，就是那些跨国技术公司基于美国的。
## 2
- The digital services tax now awaits the signature of President Emmanuel Macron, who has expressed support for the measure, and it could go into effect within the next few weeks. <br>数字服务税现在等待签字即总统Emmanuel Macron的，他已表示支持对这项措施，并且该措施可能生效于接下来的几周内。
- But it has already sparked significant controversy, with the United States trade representative opening an investigation into whether the tax discriminates against American companies, which in tum could lead to trade sanctions against France.<br>但它已经引发了极大的争议,随着美国贸易代表开始一个调查关于是否这项税歧视美国公司，这反过来可能导致贸易制裁来反抗法国。
## 3
- The French tax is not just a unilateral move by one country in need of revenue.<br>法国的这项税收不仅仅是一个单边行动仅由一个国家所需要这项收入的。
- Instead, the digital services tax is part of a much larger trend, with countries over the past few years proposing or putting in place an alphabet soup of new international tax provisions.<br>相反，数字服务税是一部分关于一个更大的趋势,随着各个国家在过去几年里提议或实施一系列的新的国际税收规定。

- They have included Britain’s DPT. (diverted profits tax), Australia’s MAAL (multinational anti-avoidance law), and India’s SEP (significant economic presence)test, to name but a few. <br>他们包括英国的DPT（转移利润税）、澳大利亚的MAAL（跨国反-避税法）和印度的SEP（显著经济存在）测试,仅说名字但不止这些。
- At the same time, the European Union, Spain, Britain and several other countries have all seriously contemplated digital services taxes.<br>与此同时，欧洲联盟、西班牙、英国和数个其他国家曾都认真考虑过数字服务税。
## 4
- These unilateral developments differ in their specifics, but they are all designed to tax multinationals on income and revenue that countries believe they should have a right to tax, even if international tax rules do not grant them that right. <br>这些单边的发展不同在他们的具体细节上，但它们都旨在征税对那些跨国公司在收入和收益上，以至于这些国家相信它们应该有一个权力去征税，即使国际税收规则没有授予它们这个权利。
- In other words, they all share a view that the international tax system has failed to keep up with the current economy.<br>换句话说，他们都分享了一个观点也就是国际税收体系已经失败了对于要赶上当前的经济体。
## 5
- In response to these many unilateral measures, the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD)is currently working with 131 countries to reach a consensus by the end of 2020 on an international solution. <br>为了回应这许多的单边措施，经济合作与发展组织（OECD）目前正在合作于131 个国家，希望达成共识在2020 年底前以找到一个国际解决方案。
- Both France and the United States are involved in the organization's work, but France's digital services tax and the American response raise questions about what the future holds for the international tax system.<br>法国和美国都参与了这个组织的工作，但是法国的数字服务税和美国的反应打上了问号关于未来走向也就是国际税收体系的。
## 6
- France’s planned tax is a clear warning:Unless a broad consensus can be reached on reforming the international tax system, other nations are likely to follow suit, and American companies will face a cascade of different taxes from dozens of nations that will prove burdensome and costly.<br>法国的计划征税是一个明确的警告：除非一个广泛的共识可以被达成以改革国际税务体系，否则其他国家很可能会跟着做，而美国公司将面临一连串的不同税收来自数十个国家的，这项税将变得繁重和昂贵的。
