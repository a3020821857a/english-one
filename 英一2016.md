# text1 时尚业的身材典范面临挑战
## 1
- France, which prides itself as the global innovator of fashion, has decided its fashion industry has lost an absolute right to define physical beauty for women. <br>法国，自豪地将自己视为全球创新者(关于时尚),已决定它的时尚产业失去了绝对的权利去定义物理美为女性.
- Its lawmakers gave preliminary approval last week to a law that would make it a crime to employ ultra-thin models on runways. <br>其立法者给予了初步批准(在上周)对一项法律(该法律使之成为一种犯罪对于雇佣超瘦模特在T台上)
- The parliament also agreed to ban websites that “incite excessive thinness”by promoting extreme dieting. <br>议会还同意禁止“煽动过度瘦弱”的网站，这些网站通过推广极端节食来促进这一现象。议会还同意禁止网站,即那些煽动过度瘦身(通过推广极端的节食)
## 2
- Such measures have a couple of uplifting motives. <br>这些措施有几个积极的动机。
- They suggest beauty should not be defined by looks that end up impinging on health. <br>它们表明，美丽不应该被定义由外表(结束于妨碍健康)。
- That’s a start. <br>这是一个开始
- And the ban on ultra-thin models seems to go beyond protecting models from starving themselves to death –as some have done.<br>并且这个禁令(阵对超瘦模特)似乎不仅仅是保护模特,免于把他们自己饿死,正如一些人所做的那样
- It tells the fashion industry that it must take responsibility for the signal it sends women, especially teenage girls, about the social tape-measure they must use to determine their individual worth. <br>它告诉时尚产业必须对其向女性，尤其是青少年女孩，发送的信号承担责任，关于她们必须用来确定个人价值的社会尺度。它告诉时尚产业,它必须承担责任对这个信号(传递给女性，尤其是青少年女孩)，关于社会尺度(她们用来衡量他们个人的价值)。
## 3
- The bans, if fully enforced, would suggest to women (and many men)that they should not let others be arbiters of their beauty. <br>这些禁令,如果完全执行，将表明向女性(和许多男性),他们不应该让其他人成为仲裁者对于他们的美丽。
- And perhaps faintly, they hint that people should look to intangible qualities like character and intellect rather than dieting their way to size zero or wasp-waist physiques.<br>而且或许微弱地，它们暗示人们应该更关注无形的品质，如性格和智力，而不是通过节食达到零号或蜂腰身材。而且可能微弱地，他们暗示:人们应该关注无形品质像品格和智慧，而不是通过节食的方法去达到零号或蜂腰的身材。
## 4
- The French measures, however, rely too much on severe punishment to change a culture that still regards beauty as skin-deep and bone-showing. <br>然而，法国的措施过于依赖严厉惩罚来改变仍然将美丽视为肤浅——甚至骨感的文化。法国的措施,然而,依赖太多于严厉的惩罚, 来改变一个文化(仍视美为肤浅的,和露骨的)。
- Under the law, using a fashion model that does not meet a government-defined index of body mass could result in a $85,000 fine and six months in prison. <br>根据法律，使用一个时尚模特(不符合政府定义的指数有关体重)可能导致85,000 美元的罚款和六个月的监禁。
## 5
- The fashion industry knows it has an inherent problem in focusing on material adornment and idealized body types.<br>时尚产业知道它存在固有的问题在关注物质上的装饰和理想化身材上。
- In Denmark, the United States, and a few other countries, it is trying to set voluntary standards for models and fashion images that rely more on peer pressure for enforcement. <br>在丹麦、美国和一些其他国家，它正在尝试设置自愿的标准为模特和时尚形象(这依赖大多是同行压力来执行)。
## 6
- In contrast to France’s actions, Denmark’s fashion industry agreed last month on rules and sanctions regarding the age, health, and other characteristics of models.<br>相比于法国的行动，丹麦的时尚产业达成一致(在上个月)制订了规则和制裁对于年龄、健康和其他特征关于模特。
- The newly revised Danish Fashion Ethical Charter clearly states:“We are aware of and take responsibility for the impact the fashion industry has on body ideals, especially on young people.”<br>新修订的丹麦时尚道德宪章明确表示：“我们意识到并承担责任对这种影响(时尚产业已经施加在身体理想上的，尤其是对年轻人的)。
- The charter’s main tool of enforcement is to deny access for designers and modeling agencies to Copenhagen Fashion Week (CFW), which is run by the Danish Fashion Institute. <br>宪章的主要工具用于执行的是拒绝参与对于那些设计师和模特机构去哥本哈根时装周（CFW）(组织于丹麦时尚学院)。
- But in general it relies on a name-and-shame method of compliance.”<br>但总的来说，它依赖公开指责的方法关于遵守。
## 7
- Relying on ethical persuasion rather than law to address the misuse of body ideals may be the best step. <br>依赖道德劝说而不是法律来解决滥用(关于身体理想)可能是最好的一步。
- Even better would be to help elevate notions of beauty beyond the material standards of a particular industry. <br>甚至更好的是去帮助提升观念关于美丽，超越物质上的标准于这个特定的行业。

# text3 光环效应：行善政策能在企业被诉时有所助益
## 1
- “There is one and only one social responsibility of business,”wrote Milton Friedman, a Nobel prize-winning economist, “That is, to use its resources and engage in activities designed to increase its profits.”<br>“唯一且只有一个的社会责任对于企业，”写自米尔顿·弗里德曼,诺贝尔奖获奖经济学家,"那就是利用其资源并从事活动,旨在增加其利润"
- But even if you accept Friedman’s premise and regard corporate social responsibility (CSR)policies as a waste of shareholders’money, things may not be absolutely clear-cut. <br>但即使你接受弗里德曼的前提并视企业社会责任（CSR）政策为一种浪费对于股东的金钱，事情可能并不是绝对地一刀切。
- New research suggests that CSR may create monetary value for companies –at least when they are prosecuted for corruption. <br>新的研究表明，CSR 可能创造货币价值为公司——至少(在他们被起诉因为腐败时)是这样。
## 2
- The largest firms in America and Britain together spend more than $15 billion a year on CSR, according to an estimate by EPG, a consulting firm.<br>这些最大的公司位于美国和英国花费超过150 亿美元每年在CSR上,根据一项估计由EPG,一家咨询公司.
- This could add value to their businesses in three ways. <br>这可以增加价值为他们的企业通过这三种方式。
- First, consumers may take CSR spending as a “signal”that a company’s products are of high quality.<br>首先，消费者可能将CSR 开支视为一种“信号”，表明一个公司产品是高质量的。
- Second, customers may be willing to buy a company’s products as an indirect way to donate to the good causes it helps. <br>第二，顾客可能愿意购买一个公司的产品,作为一种间接方式去捐赠给这种好事业由于它曾帮助过。
- And third, through a more diffuse “halo effect,”whereby its good deeds earn it greater consideration from consumers and others. <br>第三，通过更广泛的“光环效应”，即其善行为其赢得了更多的体谅来自消费者和其他人。
## 3
- Previous studies on CSR have had trouble differentiating these effects because consumers can be affected by all three. <br>以前的研究(关于企业社会责任（CSR）)遇到了困难(在区分这些影响方面)
- A recent study attempts to separate them by looking at bribery prosecutions under America’s Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA).<br>一项最近的研究试图区分它们通过研究贿赂起诉在美国的《海外腐败行为法案》（FCPA）下。
- It argues that since prosecutors do not consume a company’s products as part of their investigations, they could be influenced only by the halo effect.<br>它认为，由于检察官不会消费一个公司的产品作为一部分关于他们的调查，他们受影响仅被光环效应。
## 4
- The study found that, among prosecuted firms, those with the most comprehensive CSR programmes tended to get more lenient penalties. <br>这项研究发现，在被起诉的公司中，那些拥有最全面的CSR 划的公司倾向于得到更温和的惩罚。
- Their analysis ruled out the possibility that it was firms’political influence, rather than their CSR stand, <br>that accounted for the leniency:<br>他们的分析排除了这种可能性：是公司的政治影响力，而不是他们的CSR 立场，造成了这种宽大处理
- Companies that contributed more to political campaigns<br>did not receive lower fines. <br>公司(贡献更多对政治活动)没有收到更低的罚款。
## 5
- In all, the study concludes that <br>whereas prosecutors should only evaluate a case based on its merits,<br>they do seem to be influenced by a company’s record in CSR. <br>总的来说,一项研究总结:尽管检察官应该仅评估一个案例仅基于它的本身特征, 但是他们似乎被影响了由一个公司的记录(在CSR)
- “We estimate that either eliminating a substantial labour-rights concern, such as child labour, or increasing corporate giving by about 20%results in fines that generally are 40%lower than the typical punishment for bribing foreign officials,”says one researcher.<br>“我们估计，无论是消除一个重大的劳工权利权纠纷，如童工，还是增加企业捐赠大约20%，通常会降低40%相比典型的惩罚对于贿赂外国官员”，说于一位研究员。
## 6
- Researchers admit that their study does not answer the question of how much businesses ought to spend on CSR. <br>研究人员承认，他们的研究并未解答这个问题(即企业应该花费多少在CSR上)。
- Nor does it reveal how much companies are banking on the halo effect, rather than the other possible benefits, when they decide their do-gooding policies. <br>它也未能揭示出企业能多大程度指望光环效应,而不是其他可能的益处,当公司决定其善行政策时。
- But at least they have demonstrated that when companies get into trouble with the law, evidence of good character can win them a less costly punishment.<br>但至少他们已经证明，当公司有麻烦在法律上时,证据关于良好的品格可以为他们赢得较低成本的惩罚。
